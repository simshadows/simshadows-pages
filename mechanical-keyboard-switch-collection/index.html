Sim's Mechanical Keyboard Switch Collection

This page is a visual guide on what switches I own in my collection. It's made for my own benefit to help me identify and compare, but I made it a public webpage since there's no reason to keep it to myself. I hope you will find this useful!

For now, I only collect stock MX-style mechanical switches. I'll update this if I get around to lubing, filming, or building frankenswitches.

If you think I've made any mistakes, please open an issue on GitHub.

Note: I need to redo the "SMD" column. I currently assume a cutout means light can pass through well. Although I do mark switches that have LED cutouts, they don't guarantee good transmission of SMD lighting.

Primary identification columns:

  • Type: Either Linear, Tactile, or Clicky.
  • Image: An image that I believe best-represents my sample switch's appearance in-person, and best at helping tell apart different switches.
    • I prefer sharp, well-lit photos that show the bottom-housing, top-housing, and stem. Colours should be as clear as possible.
    • These images are not my own. They were pulled from various places on the internet. For original image sources, click on the images.
  • Names: The most-specific phrases I can find that best identifies a switch.
    • Most of the time, I'll likely only be able to use generic names based on what others call them, but the goal is to narrow down "series names", model numbers, round numbers, etc. as much as possible.
    • It should be assumed that all switches have some chance of being misidentified, misnamed, or even be fakes.
    • Switches that I believe are especially uncertain in identification will be marked accordingly. Switches with such a marking should be assumed to have uncertain naming and documented characteristics.
  • Cosmetic Variant: (self-explanatory)
    • (I'm considering removing this column. Sometimes, even if something is a cosmetic variant, I feel like they're better off having a separate "name".)

Additional identification columns for useful visual characteristics that assist in telling apart different switches:

  • Top Label: Moulded label at the top of the switch. These values are case-sensitive to best-represent the font used for the label.
  • SMD: SMD LED compatibility.
    • If Cutout, then there is a cutout to allow light to shine through.
    • If Transparent, then there is no cutout but light should be able to shine through.
    • If Semi-Transparent, then there is no cutout and light might be able to shine through, but may be more heavily-attenuated than a truly transparent housing.
    • If No, then there is no cutout and the bottom housing is opaque, which isn't designed to let light through.
  • Pins: Either 3-pin or 5-pin.
  • Additional Identification Notes: Any other interesting features.

Documented characteristics columns are for further features of the switch that have been documented in various places (such as official datasheets, store pages, blog posts, etc.):

  • Interested Documented Characteristics: The most useful summary I can find that describes what sets the switch apart from other switches (apart from just visual).
    • Terms used can mean different things between different sources. If I feel a term is well-defined, it will be typeset as strong. If I feel a term is vaguely defined or imprecise, it will be typeset as emphasis. Unintuitive but well-defined terms I will use to the best of my ability:
      • Operating force: Peak force required to reach the operating position.
      • Pretravel: Distance between rest and the operating position.
      • Total travel: Distance between rest and bottom-out.
      • (Operating position: Position in which the switch registers a change of signal.)
    • Documentation found may not be accurate or free of typos. To help, I have included citations for all information found.

Sample identification columns identify specific samples in my collection.

  • Sample Count: The number of samples I have.
  • Origin: An ID for the origin, consisting of the purchase date, where I got it from, and the region it's from.
  • Underside Labels: Labels found on the underside of the switch.
    • I'm actually not entirely sure what these are, but I expect they're for identifying moulds, batches, etc.
    • I record this information because it helps me tell apart different samples of the same switch type.
  • Item Cost (Per Switch): The price I purchased the individual switch for, before shipping and fees.
    • Since I'm usually buying switch sample packs or low-quantities of individual switches, these prices are often more expensive compared to buying a large enough quantity to fill a keyboard.
    • These prices are usually inferred, but all the individual costs will always add up to exactly how much I paid for them. For example, if I got a $10 sample pack consisting of two switches "Switch A" and "Switch B", if the store sells Switch A at $30 per pack of 10 and Switch B at $10 per pack of 10, I will infer that my Switch A costed $7.50 and my Switch B costed $2.50.
    • Includes tax related to the item cost.
  • Shipping/Fees (Per Switch): The price I paid for shipping and fees for the individual switch.
    • Similar to Item Cost, this price is inferred but will always add up to exactly how much I paid for it. For example, if we use the Switch A and Switch B examples from earlier, if shipping costed $5 in total for the entire sample pack, then Switch A is inferred to have a shipping cost of $3.75 while Switch B will be inferred to have a shipping cost of $1.25.
    • Includes tax related to the shipping cost.
  • Comments: Additional comments about the sample.
